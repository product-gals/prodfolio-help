The About section tells visitors who you are as a product manager. It's your chance to go beyond the resume and share what makes you *you*. 💬

## Professional summary

A concise overview of your background and what you bring to the table as a product manager. Think of it as your elevator pitch — but better, because you actually have time to write it. 😄

## Expertise areas

Select from predefined areas that match your product management experience:

- Platform PM, Growth PM, AI/ML PM, Technical PM, Data PM
- Consumer PM, B2B PM, Subscriptions/DTC, Payments

You can also add **custom expertise tags** for areas not in the list. This helps hiring managers quickly see what kind of PM you are.

## Product philosophy

A longer-form section (up to 2,000 characters) where you can share your approach to product management. We recommend 300–500 words covering:

- How you think about building products
- What principles guide your product decisions
- What makes you unique as a product manager

<Tip>Your product philosophy is what differentiates you from other PMs with similar titles. Use it to show how you think, not just what you've done. This is the part of your PM portfolio that people remember.</Tip>

## Customizing section titles

On your published portfolio, the philosophy section is titled **"My Product Philosophy"** by default. You can rename it inline — hover over the title while viewing your live portfolio to edit.
